About

I'm a full-stack software engineer at Valcom, where I work on emergency command-and-control web applications — where a dispatcher needs the dashboard to stay responsive during an active incident. Before Valcom, I taught full-stack web development and cybersecurity at Coding Dojo for three years while building the foundation I use today. Before that, I studied theater at San Diego State, which is where I learned how to walk into a room of strangers, hold attention, and explain something clearly. Turns out that's most of senior engineering.

How I work with AI

Vibe coding is not a thing. I delegate syntax and typing to Claude and Cursor — the architecture, the plan, and the quality of the result are my job. I won't commit code I can't explain to another engineer.

In practice: I write specs before I open an AI tool. I review every suggestion against the actual requirements. I catch the cases where the model is confident and wrong, because I know what the system is supposed to do. AI is a speed multiplier on mechanical work. It doesn't replace the person who has to own the system and answer for it six months later.
